# 03 — VOICE & TONE PLAYBOOK
*Axial Labs · v1.0 · 2026-05-07*

This document is the operating manual for **how Axial Labs sounds** — in marketing, in product, in proposals, in DMs, in error states. It is also the canonical reference Claude Sonnet uses to generate copy.

---

## 1. CORE VOICE (constant across every channel)

Six adjectives, in priority order:

1. **Precise** — exact words, exact numbers.
2. **Confident** — declarative, no hedging.
3. **Restrained** — fewer words is always better.
4. **Technical** — comfortable with code, infrastructure, and craft vocabulary.
5. **Human** — never robotic. Warm in conversation, dry in product.
6. **Opinionated** — we have a stance and we say it.

### What this voice is NOT
Not playful. Not winking. Not "fun." Not corporate. Not aspirational-vague. Not motivational. Not salesy.

---

## 2. TONE (varies by context)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage | Declarative, minimal | "Systems for ambitious software." |
| About / Manifesto | Editorial, slightly longer sentences | "Most agencies hand you a PDF. We hand you a repository." |
| Product UI | Functional, often Mono | `BUILD COMPLETE · 2.4s` |
| Error states | Calm, specific, useful | "Token build failed at line 47. Missing semicolon in color.json." |
| Social posts | Direct, claim-driven | "The kerning is the work." |
| Proposals | Warm, structured, specific | "We'll ship a tokenized identity in four weeks. Here's how." |
| Internal docs | Technical, precise, complete | "All assets reference tokens by name. Never inline values." |
| Email / DMs | Conversational, brief | "Sounds good. Sending the brief tomorrow." |

The voice never changes. The **tone** flexes between *editorial* (marketing) and *operational* (product).

## 3. THE AXIAL VOICE LAWS

### Law 1 — Specifics over slogans
Every claim must include a number, a name, a tool, or a verb. If it could appear on any agency's website, rewrite it.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "Lightning-fast delivery" | "Ships in 4 weeks" |
| "We help brands grow" | "We doubled their organic signups in six months" |
| "Cutting-edge technology" | "Built on Postgres, Next.js, and Tailwind" |
| "Best-in-class design" | "Awarded Site of the Day on Awwwards" |

### Law 2 — Verbs over adjectives
Cut adjectives. Lead with verbs.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "Beautiful, modern, intuitive interfaces" | "Interfaces you can navigate in five seconds" |
| "Powerful design system" | "A design system you can ship to production" |
| "Sleek and responsive" | "Renders cleanly on any screen" |

### Law 3 — Concrete over abstract
Name the tool, the file, the framework, the metric.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "We use modern tools" | "We use Linear, Figma, GitHub, and Vercel" |
| "Our process is rigorous" | "Every commit passes a 12-point QA checklist" |
| "Robust foundations" | "Tokens in W3C format, built with Style Dictionary" |

### Law 4 — Brevity is the brand
If you can cut a word, cut it. If you can cut a sentence, cut it.

| Long | Tight |
|---|---|
| "We are a design studio that specializes in helping companies build their brand identities and digital products from the ground up." | "We design brands and the products they live in." |
| "Get in touch with us today to learn more." | "Get in touch." |

### Law 5 — Stance, never neutral
Have a position. Say it.

- We prefer borders to shadows.
- We don't do rounded corners on cards.
- We don't ship logos without tokens.
- We don't run on RFPs.

## 4. FORBIDDEN VOCABULARY

Never use these words on the website, in proposals, in posts, or in product copy.

**Adjectives:** innovative · cutting-edge · best-in-class · world-class · seamless · robust · powerful · dynamic · vibrant · engaging · stunning · beautiful (for our own work — clients can call it that, we don't).

**Verbs:** empower · unlock · leverage · streamline · supercharge · revolutionize · transform · disrupt · elevate · enable.

**Nouns:** synergy · solutions · ecosystem (when we mean "tools") · journey (unless literal) · stakeholder · alignment (unless geometric).

**Phrases:** "let's chat" · "drop us a line" · "we're passionate about…" · "at the intersection of…" · "thought leader" · "next-generation" · "AI-powered" (we are AI-native, that's different).

**Punctuation we avoid:** exclamation marks (one per page max) · semicolons (rare) · em dashes (use sparingly, prefer periods) · all caps in body copy (Mono only).

**Nouns:** axis · system · token · repo · grid · pixel · component · template · primitive · pipeline · contract · interface · surface · layer · module · spec.

**Adjectives** (use sparingly): precise · structured · tokenized · versionable · regeneratable · modular · grid-aligned · production-grade · canonical.

## 6. SENTENCE PATTERNS

### Pattern A — The Declaration
Short. Subject-verb-object. Period.

> *We design brands.*
> *We ship the software.*
> *Tokens beat colors.*

### Pattern B — The Contrast
Two sentences. The first names the wrong way; the second names ours.

> *Most studios deliver files. We deliver repositories.*
> *Most agencies hand off. We ship.*

### Pattern C — The Spec
Number, unit, claim.

> *Four weeks to launch.*
> *Twelve QA points per asset.*
> *One token, every surface.*

### Pattern D — The Editorial Pair
Used only in long-form. A claim, a beat of texture, then a payoff.

> *The brand isn't the logo. It isn't the typeface, the color, the deck. It's the system underneath all of those — the one that decides what comes next.*

## 8. PUNCTUATION & FORMATTING

- **Periods.** Use them. Sentence fragments are allowed and encouraged.
- **Commas** — used for clarity, not rhythm.
- **Em dashes** — okay sparingly. Prefer periods.
- **Semicolons** — almost never.
- **Exclamation marks** — none. (One per quarter, maximum.)
- **Quote marks** — straight quotes in code, smart quotes in prose.
- **Numbers** — spell out one through nine, numerals from 10.
- **Capitalization** — sentence case for headlines (not Title Case). UPPERCASE only in Mono.
- **Oxford comma** — yes.
- **Ampersands** — only in lockups (e.g., "Design & Engineering"). Otherwise spell "and."

---

## 9. NAMING CONVENTIONS (sub-products, projects)

- Always two words: "Axial [Noun]."
- The second word is one syllable when possible (OS, Field, Index, Foundry, Studio).
- Capitalize each word.
- In Mono UPPERCASE for product UI.

---

## 10. WRITING ABOUT CLIENTS

- **Specific over generic.** Name the client unless under NDA.
- **Numbers over adjectives.** "Doubled signups in six months," not "drove significant growth."
- **Their voice, not ours.** Quote them when possible.
- **Process visible.** Show the system, not just the outcome.

---

## 11. WRITING ABOUT OURSELVES

- **First person plural by default** ("we").
- **First person singular** for founder/personal posts only.
- **Never refer to the team as "our team" in marketing copy** — say "we."
- **No biographical fluff.** State the work, not the journey.

---

## 12. CRISIS / SENSITIVE COMMUNICATION

- Acknowledge directly. No corporate softening.
- State what happened in factual terms.
- State what we're doing about it.
- State when we'll update next.
- No apology theater. Apologize once, mean it, move on.

---

## 13. FINAL CHECK — THE 5-WORD TEST

Before publishing any line: read it aloud. If it could fit on a competitor's website with one word swapped, **rewrite it**. Our voice is unmistakable when it's right. If you have to ask, it isn't.
